iPhone 17 Pro: Radical 'No-Button' Design & Periscope Zoom Leaked

By Alex Carter | September 17, 2025

Just as the dust settles on the iPhone 16 launch, the rumor mill is already churning at full speed for next year's flagship. And if the latest whispers from supply chain sources are to be believed, the iPhone 17 Pro could be the most significant design leap we've seen from Apple in half a decade. We're talking a complete reimagining of the iPhone's form factor, moving towards a seamless device that feels more like a single piece of polished obsidian than a collection of components.

The headline feature, according to multiple analysts, is the move to a portless, buttonless chassis. The idea of a seamless "glass box" has been an Apple design fantasy for years, but new haptic feedback technology and improved MagSafe charging speeds might finally make it a reality. Instead of physical volume and power buttons, the iPhone 17 Pro could feature solid-state haptic areas that vibrate to simulate a click, much like the Home button on older iPhones. While it sounds futuristic and would undoubtedly improve water resistance, the transition could be a headache for case manufacturers and users accustomed to the tactile feel of a real button.

"Apple's endgame has always been a perfectly uninterrupted slab of glass. The technology is finally catching up to their ambition. Expect the iPhone 17 to be a major step in that direction."

Perhaps more exciting for photographers is the long-awaited arrival of a periscope zoom lens. This technology, already present in many high-end Android phones, uses a prism to fold light at a 90-degree angle, allowing for a much longer telephoto lens to fit inside a slim phone body. This could finally push the iPhone's optical zoom capabilities from 3x to a much more competitive 10x, closing a significant gap with its rivals. This wouldn't just be for wildlife photography; imagine the clarity for portraits and everyday shots from a distance.

Powering these new features will likely be the A19 Bionic chip, which is expected to focus heavily on AI and neural processing. This could unlock a new suite of "Pro" camera features, like real-time video background removal or advanced AI-powered scene enhancement that goes far beyond what's currently possible. For gamers, the A19 could bring true console-level graphics with hardware-accelerated ray tracing, making the iPhone 17 Pro an even more formidable handheld gaming device.

Combined with a rumored brighter ProMotion display and the potential for under-display Face ID to finally eliminate the notch or Dynamic Island, the iPhone 17 Pro is shaping up to be more than just an iterative update. While these are still early-stage rumors, the convergence of multiple reliable sources suggests Apple is preparing to make a big statement. If even half of these leaks pan out, the iPhone 17 Pro could be the revolution fans have been waiting for.
